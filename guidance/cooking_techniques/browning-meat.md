# Browning meat

Spread ground beef in an **even layer** and don't crowd the pan — a packed pan steams the meat gray and sandy instead of building browned, savory crumbles. You want **brown meat, not gray meat**: work in batches if the pan is full.

For quick-cooking dishes where you want well-browned crumbles (chili, tacos, sloppy joes, stir-fries, weeknight meat sauce), pre-treat the beef with **baking soda**: dissolve ¼ tsp baking soda in 1 Tbsp water per pound, mix it in evenly, and let it rest **15 minutes** (up to an hour is fine). Raising the surface pH keeps the proteins from squeezing out moisture (juicier, not dry pebbles) and speeds the Maillard reaction (deeper browning and flavor). Especially worth it for lean blends (90/10, 93/7). Skip it for long braises and soups, where the benefit fades.
